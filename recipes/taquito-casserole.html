Taquito Casserole

Back to main page

Description

This taquito casserole, with a creamy base of beans, corn, and cheese, baked crispy taquitos on top, and a drizzle of jalapeno crema, is like an enchilada casserole but with tiny enchiladas.

Ingredients

  • 1 (15 ounce) can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 (15 ounce) can corn, drained
  • 1 (10 ounce) can red enchilada sauce
  • 1 (4 ounce) can diced green chiles
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 (20 count) package frozen taquitos
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup pickled jalapeno slices
  • 1 tablespoon juice from pickled jalapeno slices

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  2. Combine black beans, corn, enchilada sauce, green chiles, 1/2 cup sour cream, Mexican cheese blend, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper in a large bowl. Stir in 1/4 cup cilantro.
  3. Spread bean mixture into the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Arrange taquitos on top.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven until bubbly, about 30 minutes.
  5. While casserole is baking, combine 1/2 cup sour cream, 1/4 cup cilantro, jalapeno slices, and juice in a blender. Blend until smooth.
  6. Drizzle jalapeno crema over the casserole before serving.
